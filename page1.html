Just Dance

Just Dance is a motion-based dancing game for multiple players, with each game including a collection of classic and modern songs each with its own dance choreographies. During each song, players mirror a dance performed by actors on the screen, following commands that appear on the screen, and are awarded for their accuracy. Additionally, there are gold moves in which players must strike a pose in order to earn bonus points. Players are given ranks based on how well they do. Depending on the game and system it is played on, the game can be played with either motion controllers or camera devices (such as the Wii Remote and Kinect), or using an app downloaded on a smart device.

Dance in life

Dancing often becomes a way of life for people. It is no longer two or three hours a week spent in the dance hall, but something more. Dance lovers start listening to the music that is specific to the style of dance they are doing. They start watching videos from tournaments, educational and feature films about dance, go to dance parties and battles. Over time, life without dance becomes difficult to imagine.

One of the obvious pluses of dancing is its unquestionable benefit for health. Trained and strengthened not only the muscles, but also the internal systems and organs that begin to go haywire if a person leads a passive lifestyle. Dancing contributes to good posture and graceful gait, speed up metabolism, train the vestibular apparatus, which is certainly useful in everyday life, and even more so when doing extreme sports (water or winter skiing, skydiving, etc.).
